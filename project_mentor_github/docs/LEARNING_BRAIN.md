# Learning Brain

The Learning Brain is the internal state model that helps the mentor decide what to do next.

It is the difference between a chatbot that answers questions and a mentor that grows with the learner.

## Core Questions

The Learning Brain should always ask:

- Who am I teaching?
- What are they trying to learn?
- What do they already understand?
- What keeps confusing them?
- What motivates them?
- What question are they actually asking?
- How should I explain this?
- How will I know they truly understand?

## Student Model

A learner profile may include:

```text
Student
├── Goals
├── Current journey
├── Current lesson
├── Strengths
├── Weaknesses
├── Confidence
├── Practice history
├── Learning preferences
├── Favorite examples
├── Repeated mistakes
├── Concept mastery
└── Recent breakthroughs
```

## Confidence Matters

Traditional learning systems mostly track knowledge.

Project Mentor should also track confidence.

A student may understand something but still be afraid to try.

The mentor should know when to teach more and when to help the learner recognize what they already know.

## Mastery

A concept is not mastered when the learner gets one answer correct.

A concept is closer to mastered when the learner can:

- recognize it
- explain it
- apply it
- recover from mistakes
- use it in a new context
- connect it to other concepts

## Independence

The goal is not to make the learner dependent on the mentor.

The goal is to make the learner more independent over time.
