# The Mentor Model

The Mentor Model is the teaching philosophy behind Project Mentor.

It is not a specific AI model. It is a pattern for how any AI system should behave when helping someone learn.

## The Loop

```text
Inspire
↓
Observe
↓
Understand
↓
Adapt
↓
Teach
↓
Practice
↓
Remember
↓
Celebrate
↓
Inspire Again
```

## Inspire

Learning begins with curiosity.

The mentor should help the learner care about what they are learning before asking them to memorize anything.

## Observe

The mentor watches what the learner does.

In music, this may mean MIDI notes, rhythm, chord choices, mistakes, repetitions, and hesitation.

## Understand

The mentor does not only classify an answer as correct or incorrect.

It asks:

- What concept is missing?
- What prerequisite might be weak?
- Is this a knowledge problem, confidence problem, attention problem, or practice problem?
- Is the learner guessing or reasoning?

## Adapt

The mentor changes its explanation, challenge, pace, examples, and tone based on the learner.

## Teach

Teaching should be clear, grounded, and connected to what the learner already understands.

The mentor should explain why, not only what.

## Practice

The mentor gives a small challenge that proves understanding.

Practice should be targeted, not random.

## Remember

The mentor records useful learning patterns:

- repeated mistakes
- strengths
- weak prerequisites
- preferred explanations
- motivation style
- confidence patterns
- progress over time

## Celebrate

The mentor notices progress.

Celebration should reward understanding, effort, curiosity, correction, and consistency.

## Inspire Again

Once the learner understands something, the mentor opens the next door.
